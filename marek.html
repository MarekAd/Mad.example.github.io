I graduated from Pozna≈Ñ university of technology. I got a master title in civil engeenering as a constructions designer. That means I could design buildings construction (not architectional issues but pure engeenering) or lead a constuction site. And I did.

I liked solving mechanical problems by several theories and see how it works out of Autocad's drawing board, so I hired to project company. Unfortunately, for me, the job turned out to be quite boring and not satisfying because of it's recurrence. I decided to go into the battle... I hired to general contractor of one of the biggest factories in Poland.

I liked working under time pressure and all the resposibilities connected with beeing a site engineer but I hated to be far from home. Actually I hate cold too and building open construction during winter was quite fun for an adventure/ but I couldn't imagine doing that for a lifetime. I have to admit that I really liked contract issuess so I became kind of contract engineer, responsibile for claims and performance of the contract. It was challenging and addicting, but I realized that I'm effective because of mathematical analysess and self-developed excel tools... So bearing in mind everything I've learnd in the field of soft skills I asked myself why not to became a coder? :)
